Walmart

An accessibility integration and simplified interface to assist elderly online shoppers.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

The Walmart Easy.Access is an accessibility integration that was proposed to be added onto the current Walmart e-commerce website. It would provide accessibility options to users (particularly elderly users) on the Walmart website and also come with a built-in and toggleable simplified redesigned interface to help those elderly users navigate the site more easily. Based on current research and data, I developed mock-ups of an accessibility integration called Easy.Access that would work alongside the Walmart website.

Problem : With an 18% increase in technology ownership among elderly individuals in the last 4 years and 57% of elderly individuals over the age of 65 reporting finding online shopping sites difficult to navigate, along with individuals over the age of 65 having the highest discretionary income available out of any generation, these elderly shoppers are an underrepresented market that could potentially serve as a large customer base for Walmart.

Goal : Give elderly shoppers better access to the online Walmart site to help Walmart generate more revenue.

This project was conducted as a part of the capstone project of the Accenture Learning 2 Lead internship. We chose an Accenture diamond client, and proposed a solution to a problem we noticed they were facing.

Research

Walmart generates $441 billion dollars annually from their in-person stores.

20% of these shoppers are above the age of 65.

Over 1 in 5 Americans over the age of 65 are not driving, meaning they aren’t able to get to in-person stores by themselves.

With Walmart’s current accessibility layout, some of these potential customers cannot fully take advantage of the website.


This means that Walmart could be missing out on upwards of $22 billion dollars annually from this demographic alone.

User personas, based on Walmart’s elderly shopper demographics.

The solution: Easy.Access

Easy.Access is an accessibility integration to help improve shopping experiences for elderly users and those with physical disabilities or impairments. It will also be compliant with accessibility guidelines such as WCAG 2.2, the ADA, Section 508, and AODA.

Features:
◈ High contrast mode
◈ Color/vibrancy controls
◈ Font/text size controls
◈ Voice commands
◈ Cursor size
◈ Ability to purchase items through phone without needing to click or scroll often
◈ Easy Access Mode, a completely revamped interface designed for simple user and navigation

General Design Goals: Larger text, simpler navigation, larger content, cleaner (less cluttered) feel.

Results

◈ Conducted secondary research, working with existing statistics to identify a lack in accessibility options, particularly for elderly users

◈ Designed and prototyped an accessibility integration, along with a simplified and accessible interface by applying WCAG, ADA, Section 508, and AODA guidelines on age-related impairments. Major improvements included increased design consistency and simplification, fewer needed interactions, and larger interface elements

◈ Proposed a marketing strategy to promote this integration through branded truck trailers, YouTube ads, and physical delivery packages

◈ If used to its fullest extent, the feature was expected to contribute over $20 billion in annual revenue to Walmart through expanding reach to an under-served market of customers

◈ Presented the final prototype and solution, winning 1st place in the 2024 Accenture Learning 2 Lead Summer Capstone Symposium

Reflection

◈ This project showed me the value of accessibility, that it’s not just a feature or element of design, but a necessity. Even seemingly small things, such as a couple pixel different in font size or spacing, can have a large influence on usability, especially for elderly users.

◈ If time allowed, I would have conducted direct interviews with older adults to gain a better grasp on their perspective, instead of relying mostly on secondary research. I would have also conducted usability tests to identify areas that need improvement, as small changes are always significant.

◈ Completing this project also reminds me that accessible design doesn’t mean making things dull or out-of-place to accommodate. It means keeping things both focused and aligned with user needs.
