Messages

A video call integration concept for the Google Messages app, when only Google Duo existed.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Google Messages is a messaging app for Android users to send texts. It has options to call, text, upload media, and more, like many other texting apps. However, the app does not feature native video calling; it could only be done through Google Duo, which many people have never used or rarely use. I conducted end-to-end UX research and design to develop an idea of what integrated video calling would look like within Google Messages.

Problem : Android users who use Google Messages as their default texting app do not have a convenient way of video calling.

Goal : Allow for Android users to have quick access to integrated video calling and visualize what that may look like in a way that addresses common concerns.

This project was conducted as the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill App Team Carolina UI/UX Bootcamp final project.

ROLE

Solo UX Designer

ANALYSIS

Many other messaging applications have Integrated Video Calls. Google Messages does not, which can hinder users from using the application for the lack of this feature.

They may be more prone to switch to something like WhatsApp, but it may be slightly more inconvenient for users because it does not work with SMS/MMS/RCS messaging. For Android users, there is not a “best” solution.

Results

◈ Prototyped a mockup design for how a video calling feature may look within the Google Messages Material UI, eliminating the need for an external app like Google Duo

◈ Prioritized accessibility and ease of use, placing video calls within one interaction from a contact conversation

◈ Expected impact includes an increased use of the Google Messages app and faster access to both calls and video calls

◈ Presented the final prototype at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill App Team Carolina UI/UX Bootcamp as a final capstone

Reflection

◈ I learned how to design within the constraints of an existing design system while introducing a new feature; in this case, with Google’s Material UI and adding video calling.

◈ I practiced finding a balance between creativity and consistency to ensure that the video calling feature felt like it was really part of the app.

◈ If I had more time or expanded on this project, I’d conduct usability tests with both Android and iOS users to collect their opinions on what could be changed or improved. I would also have conducted more user interviews, including with iOS users to understand what concerns they have about FaceTime and what makes it intuitive, reliable, and practical.
